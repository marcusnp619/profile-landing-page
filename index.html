⚖️ Legal Landscape

🇺🇸 United States

The CFAA (Computer Fraud and Abuse Act) governs unauthorized access. Authorized penetration testing with written permission is legal.

"2022 DOJ memorandum clarifies that good-faith security research does not violate CFAA when authorized by the system owner."

🇪🇺 European Union

GDPR and NIS Directive regulate security testing. Responsible disclosure and written authorization are essential for compliance.

EU frameworks emphasize responsible vulnerability handling and data protection during security assessments.

🌍 International

Laws vary by jurisdiction. Always obtain written authorization, follow responsible disclosure practices, and maintain detailed documentation.

International best practice: Establish clear rules of engagement and maintain ethical standards across all security research activities.
